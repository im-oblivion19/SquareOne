About SquareOne

SquareOne explores the connection between origami, mathematics, geography, and design. The project uses an interactive world map to show where major origami-related mathematical ideas came from and how each one connects to folding, structure, and geometry.

Website Timeline

Step 1

Concept

The project began as an idea to connect origami with mathematical discovery across different parts of the world.

Step 2

Research

The team selected key theorems and mathematical ideas, including Kawasaki's theorem, Beloch's fold, Lang's tree theorem, the fold-and-cut theorem, and Gaussian curvature.

Step 3

Map Design

The interactive map was built with country pins, hover boxes, and short descriptions for each theorem location.

Step 4

Final Website

The final version added styled information cards, a cleaner layout, and an about page explaining the team and purpose of the project.
